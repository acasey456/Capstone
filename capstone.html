Active Recovery

Active recovery involves performing low intensity exercise after a workout to keep blood flowing and help muscles recover and rebuild. While many may opt for inactivity or completely resting after working out, it is more beneficial to have an active recovery or cooldown before completely resting. Active recovery works by reducing lactic acid buildup in muscles, eliminating toxins, keeping muscles flexible, reducing soreness, increasing blood flow, and helping you maintain your exercise routine. Some good things that can work as active recovery exercises are swimming, yoga, walking or jogging, and cycling. Through participating in active recovery you will likely find yourself less tight, sore, and more energetic. However, if you’re injured or in pain your body likely needs more passive recovery.

Rest

It’s easy to feel guilty when taking a day off from working out, but it is actually very important. Rest days are critical for a variety of reasons both physiological and psychological. Physically it allows the body to repair, rebuild and strengthen. It allows your body to adapt to the stress of exercise and replenish energy stores so that you don’t experience muscle tissue breakdown. On the psychological side it can help maintain a better work, home, and fitness balance. Sleep is also an important part of this rest. Getting inadequate sleep hinders the body from proper recovery and can have adverse physical and mental effects. It’s important to pay attention to your body and how it feels to determine how much and how often you need to build recovery and rest days into your schedule.

Stretching

Stretching keeps muscles flexible, strong, and healthy so it’s important to incorporate it into any fitness routine. This flexibility is important to maintain a range of motion in the joints. Without this, muscles shorten and become tight, increasing your risk of injury. It’s also important to remember to warm up before stretching otherwise you can injure yourself because the muscle fibers aren’t prepared. Warming up can involve a light jog or activity so that your muscles are warm and ready. For each stretch you should hold it for thirty seconds without bouncing.

Nutrition

When exercising consistently it is important to have a diet that supports this activity. Having the right diet can maximize performance and results. Proper nutrition for athletes can prevent conditions like injuries, contribute to better sleep, fuel performance, and help gain a competitive edge. Carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats allow athletes to maintain stamina and endurance. What each athlete needs usually depends on them individually so it's important to pay attention to your diet and figure out what balance works for your body. Hydration is another key part of maintaining good nutrition. Water provides musculoskeletal lubrication as well as helps to transport nutrients throughout your body which is key to your body being able to perform.
